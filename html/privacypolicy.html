COOKIES

A cookie is a small file which asks permission to be placed on your computer's hard drive. Once you agree, the file is added and the cookie helps analyze web traffic or lets you know when you visit a particular site. Cookies allow web applications to respond to you as an individual. Esper tracks cookies as part of the Internet Gathered Information.

Esper's website, Products and Services may be tailored to your needs, likes and dislikes by gathering and remembering Internet Gathered Information and Customer Information.

Esper uses traffic log cookies to identify which pages are being used. We may then analyze data about web page traffic and improve the Esper website and tailor it to individual customer needs. Esper only uses the Internet Gathered Information for statistical analysis purposes; the Internet Gather Information is removed from Esper's servers once the analysis is completed.

Overall, the Internet Gathered Information helps Esper provide you with better Products and Services by enabling us to monitor which web pages are visited the most. A cookie does not give Esper access to your computer or any information about you other than the data you choose to share with Esper. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies but you may modify your browser features to decline cookies. If cookies are declined, you may not have full access to the Esper website, Products or Services.

Esper may also collect information about your Internet Protocol ("IP") address to track and aggregate non-personal information. For example, Esper may use IP addresses to monitor the regions from which customers and visitors navigate Esper's website. IP addresses may also be used to confirm a customer's identity when the customer uses the Esper website, Products and Services.
